Sphere-tracing Raymarcher

Stormhack 2024  ·  24-hour build 1st Place

Overview

A real-time raymarcher built during a 24-hour hackathon. The renderer uses K-Lipschitz sphere tracing against a procedural FBM (Fractional Brownian Motion) terrain — achieving a 40% reduction in step count compared to naive constant-step raymarching.

The key insight: for a Lipschitz-continuous distance field with known Lipschitz constant L, each marching step can safely advance by d / L rather than a conservative fixed increment. For terrain SDFs this allows dramatically larger steps in empty regions while staying safe near surfaces.

Technical details

The terrain is defined as a height field: f(x,z) = fbm(x,z) * scale. The SDF is p.y - f(p.x, p.z), which has a known Lipschitz bound derivable from the FBM amplitude sum.

Lighting uses a simple analytic Phong model. Normals are computed via central differences with a small epsilon. Atmospheric fog is composited as a depth-based exponential.
